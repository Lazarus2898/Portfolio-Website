Lateral Movement in Active Directory

Posted on February 16, 2026 | Category: Cybersecurity

Introduction

After gaining initial access to a network, attackers rarely stop there. The next phase is typically lateral movement — the process of moving from one compromised machine to others within the environment.

In Active Directory environments, this often means escalating privileges, harvesting credentials, and pivoting across domain-joined systems.

Common Techniques

1. Pass-the-Hash

Instead of cracking passwords, attackers reuse NTLM hashes to authenticate to other systems. If local administrator passwords are reused, compromise spreads quickly.

2. Kerberoasting

Service tickets requested from domain controllers can be extracted and cracked offline to recover service account credentials.
